The Craft

From print to resin, by hand.

01

Archival print

Museum-grade pigment inks laid down on cotton-rag paper. Each piece sized to its mount before printing — no cropping, no compromises. Holds colour for a hundred years.

  • 10-colour archival pigment ink
  • 320 gsm cotton-rag substrate
  • Sized per piece — no upscaling
02

Hand-cut to shape

Each printed image is hand-cut to the exact outline of the subject — no laser, no template. A scalpel and a steady hand follow the silhouette down to a 1 mm tolerance, including fins, whiskers and tail.

  • Surgical-grade scalpel · fresh per piece
  • 1 mm tolerance to the outline
  • Edge bevelled for clean shadow
03

Mounted on timber

The cut piece is hand-laid onto a plank of Australian hardwood — Silky Oak, Spotted Gum, Blackwood or Ironbark, chosen to match the subject. Bonded with a structural archival adhesive that cures invisible.

  • Australian hardwood, sustainably sourced
  • Edge-routed and sanded to 400-grit
  • Archival adhesive — no acid bleed
04

Sealed in resin

Jewellery-grade two-part epoxy is poured in stages — flooding the mount, locking the print under a lens of glass-clear resin. Forty-eight hours to cure, then signed and numbered on the back.

  • Two-part epoxy · UV stabilised
  • Poured in 3 stages over 24 hours
  • 48 hr cure · signed & numbered
