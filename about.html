The story behind Crystal Brook.

The brand takes its name from Crystal Brook — a freshwater creek that winds out of the Atherton Tableland and runs through the cane country south of Cairns. It's the same water that gave Max the original spark: a fish, a slab of timber, and the question of how to make one feel as permanent as the other.

The studio sits on Dempsey Street in Gordonvale, half an hour's drive south of Cairns. It's a one-and-a-half-person operation — Max does the cutting, the mounting and the resin work, and his partner P. helps run the shop side. Workshop visits happen by appointment, and they're "always open" in the sense that if you're driving through and the front roller door is up, you'll usually find Max in there.

"Each one is its own piece. No two come out the same — which is the whole point."

The product range is deliberately narrow: resin-coated cut-out archival prints mounted on Australian hardwood. Five categories, no spillover into homewares or trinkets. Reef fish from the Great Barrier Reef. Freshwater natives from the rivers that feed it. Aussie-built classic cars. Working dogs, wildlife and family pets. Aussie birds, from lorikeets to wedge-tailed eagles.

What unites them is the finish — every piece spends 48 hours under jewellery-grade epoxy that locks the print, the mount and the maker's mark under a single lens of glass-clear resin. It's the kind of thing that looks expensive because it is the kind of thing that lasts a lifetime. A hundred years, if the inks are anything to go by.

The workshop

Made in Far North Queensland.

Gordonvale is a small cane town nestled at the foot of Walsh's Pyramid, on the Mulgrave River flats south of Cairns. The Bruce Highway runs straight through it. The Wet Tropics rainforest sits to the west; the Great Barrier Reef is forty minutes' drive east.

It's the kind of place where the subject matter is local and the timber's local too — Silky Oak, Spotted Gum, Blackwood and Ironbark, all sourced from QLD mills. The resin's mixed in the workshop. The prints are done in Cairns. Nothing on a Crystal Brook mount has travelled further than it had to.

The promise

Four things every mount is.

No mass production. No outsourcing. No shortcuts. Every piece that leaves the workshop carries these four guarantees.

One-of-a-kind

No two pieces identical. Each mount is hand-cut and hand-finished — fingerprint included.

Built to outlast us

Archival pigment inks rated for 100+ years. Resin-sealed. Australian hardwood. Designed to be inherited.

Made by hand

Scalpel-cut to outline, mounted on routed timber, poured in three resin stages. No CNC, no production line.

Signed & numbered

Every mount carries Max's signature on the back, plus the piece number — your record of authenticity.
