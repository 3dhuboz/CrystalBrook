Five directions for the logo

You picked the top-left emblem. Here it is alongside four refined directions — each one solves a different problem the original can't quite cover. Pick the one you want to roll out, or mix-and-match.

What we're balancing

The job the logo has to do

Sit on the website header at 32–180 px wide.

Print on a business card at the size of a thumbnail.

Work as your Instagram avatar (a circle, often viewed at 50×50).

Engrave or laser onto the back of every wall mount as a maker's stamp.

Look at home next to real photographs of your work — not compete with them.

Brands we measured against

Filson, Orvis, Drake Waterfowl — the heritage outdoor benchmark.

Patagonia, Mampara — clean modern minimalism that scales.

Yeti, Sage Fly — premium roundels that own the avatar slot.

Aesop, Maker's Mark — pure-typography brands where the wordmark IS the logo.

01

Original Emblem

Your pick. Hexagon frame, detailed bass, full heritage stamp.

Max's choice
Source · The four AI-generated concepts you sent through

What it does well

  • Beautifully detailed — feels handcrafted
  • The hexagon + bass reads instantly as "fishing brand"
  • "EST 2024" + Queensland silhouette = built-in heritage
  • Strong on dark backgrounds and merchandise

What it gives up

  • The fish detail disappears at favicon / app-icon size
  • Lots of small text in the badge — hard to print under 60mm
  • The hexagon is a "rustic-brand" trope (used by countless craft beer + leather brands)
  • Locked to one fish — doesn't represent cars / animals / birds

Best used for

Hero artwork on the homepage, packaging, and the back-of-mount maker's stamp where you have room for the full detail.

02

Heritage Premium

Filson / Orvis influence. Drops the hexagon, lets the typography do the heritage work.

Refined
V2 · Heritage Premium · gold on warm black

What it does well

  • Reads at every size — the wordmark is the anchor
  • Maker's stamp ("MAX WHITE · GORDONVALE") = real provenance
  • Same colour palette as your pick — feels like family
  • The bass is still there, just sized appropriately

What it gives up

  • Less "punch" at first glance than the hexagon emblem
  • Needs both lines to work — can't crop down to icon-only

Best used for

Website header, email signature, business card front, framed certificate-of-authenticity that ships with each mount.

03

Modern Minimalist

Mampara / Patagonia influence. Single-stroke bass, geometric wordmark, no flourish.

Clean
V3 · Modern Minimalist · ink on cream

What it does well

  • Works in pure black on white — easy to print, photocopy, fax
  • The outline bass scales from 16px to 1600px without falling apart
  • Doesn't fight your real product photography
  • Direct match to Mampara's aesthetic (the brand Max referenced)

What it gives up

  • Less "warm artisan" — more "studio brand"
  • The hand-crafted heritage cue isn't as obvious

Best used for

Website header in a minimalist layout, modern shopify-style storefront, monochrome merch (tees, caps, stickers).

04

Roundel Mark

Yeti / Sage Fly influence. Built specifically for circles — favicon, Instagram avatar, app icon.

Avatar-ready
V4 · Roundel · gold on warm black

What it does well

  • Owns the Instagram avatar slot (where most people will first see you)
  • Curved type around the bass = unmistakable "stamp" feel
  • Reads as a single mark from across a room or at 32px
  • Looks great branded onto product packaging

What it gives up

  • Curved type is hard to read at very small sizes — better as a stamp than primary header
  • Needs to be paired with a wordmark for context (V2 or V3 above)

Best used for

Favicon, app icon, social avatars (Insta / Facebook / TikTok), wax-seal stickers on packaging, the "stamp" on the back of every mount.

05

Stamp Wordmark

Aesop / Maker's Mark influence. Pure type — no fish at all. Maximum elegance.

Typographic
V5 · Stamp Wordmark · ink on cream

What it does well

  • The fish you actually sell becomes the visual — not a fish in the logo
  • Works for ALL categories (cars, birds, animals don't fit a fish-based logo)
  • Premium-as-anything — apothecary / fine-art aesthetic
  • Tiniest filesize = fastest page load

What it gives up

  • No icon — harder to "remember" at a glance
  • Loses the "fishing brand" cue some customers expect
  • Requires confident typography to feel premium not generic

Best used for

Letterhead, certificate-of-authenticity card that ships with each mount, the embossed "thank you" on the inside of packaging.
